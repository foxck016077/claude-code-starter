### Cultural Notes (Critical for closing)
```
🎯 WHY THIS MATTERS IN VIETNAM:

1. **Payment Terms** 
   - ❌ AVOID: "Net-30" or "Invoice-based" (cash flow trust issue)
   - ✅ DO: 50-50 split with clear delivery milestones
   - 💡 TIP: Vietnamese companies worry about getting paid by foreign vendors too
     → Mention you'll use escrow (Stripe, Wise, or Vietnam bank) to show trust

2. **IP Ownership**
   - ❌ AVOID: Ambiguous language ("shared ownership", "licensing")
   - ✅ DO: Crystal clear—"All code belongs to you after payment"
   - 💡 TIP: Competing developers won't steal if ownership is crystal clear

3. **Timeline & Relationship**
   - This is a COLD outreach. Vietnamese companies are relationship-first.
   - ❌ AVOID: Jumping straight to contract (looks impersonal)
   - ✅ DO: First email = introduce team + show Vietnam experience
   - 💡 HACK: Mention a local partner or familiarity with Vietnam tech ecosystem
     Example: "We've worked with [local tech company] and understand Vietnam's 
     development practices..."

4. **Red Flags to Avoid**
   - Too direct/no small talk ("Let's sign and start immediately")
   - No mention of ongoing support (makes them feel abandoned)
   - Rigid scope (they'll ask for changes mid-project)
   - Single point of contact (they need backup engineer names)

5. **What Vietnamese Companies Care About**
   - ✅ Proven references from Vietnam-region clients
   - ✅ Named engineers (not "we have a team")
   - ✅ Flexibility for mid-project scope adjustments
   - ✅ Escalation contact (in case dev goes sick)
   - ✅ Code quality metrics (test coverage, etc.)
```

### Negotiation Tips (Win-Win in Vietnam)
```
PHASE 1: Build Trust (Week 1-2)
- Email introduction with 2-3 Vietnam case studies
- Video call with your tech lead (face = trust in Vietnam)
- Share your development process + team bios
- Mention which tools you use (Django, React, etc.—they want compatibility)

PHASE 2: Scope Lockdown (Week 2-3)
- Walk through specifications screen-by-screen
- Record meeting + share notes in Vietnamese
- Ask them to sign off on each feature
- Get their 2-3 test cases in writing

PHASE 3: Contract & Payment (Week 3-4)
- Use the translated agreement above (no surprises)
- Suggest escrow service (PayPal, Wise, Stripe) for safety
- Payment schedule: 20% deposit → 30% at 50% code completion → 50% at final QA
- ✅ This gives them 4 checkpoints instead of 2 (feels more controlled)

PHASE 4: Delivery & Support (Week 4+)
- Provide code repo access (GitHub private)
- Assign one engineer as "Vietnam point of contact"
- Offer 60-day support (not just 30) for goodwill
- Follow up monthly for 6 months ("How is the app performing?")
```

### Compliance Checklist (Vietnam-Specific)
```
LEGAL & TAX
☐ Register as foreign service provider (nếu có chi nhánh tại VN)
☐ Get Vietnamese business license (if recurring revenue expected)
☐ Tax handling: 5% VAT on international services (client may request exemption)
☐ Currency: Use USD for foreign contracts (no forced VND conversion)
☐ Payment: Bank transfer via SWIFT preferred (crypto not recognized)

INDUSTRY PRACTICES
☐ NDA: Standard (they'll require bilateral, not one-way)
☐ IP Assignment: Use separate IP clause (not embedded in payment terms)
☐ Liability Cap: 2x contract value (industry standard)
☐ Escrow Account: For contracts >$30k (highly recommended)
☐ Dispute Resolution: English law preferred (Vietnamese courts slow)

RELATIONSHIP MAINTENANCE
☐ Invoice in English + Vietnamese summary
☐ Monthly status emails (even if on-track)
☐ Birthday greetings to key contact (yes, really—relationship culture)
☐ Year-end gift small token (coffee from your region—builds loyalty)
```
